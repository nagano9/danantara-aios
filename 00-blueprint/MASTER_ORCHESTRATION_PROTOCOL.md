# Danantara Master Orchestration Protocol

## End-to-end control flow

1. **Intake** — capture objective, user, entity, decision, deadline, intended audience, and requested action.
2. **Classify** — determine entity, materiality, risk tier, data classification, market sensitivity, and national-interest sensitivity.
3. **Authorize** — identify mandate, decision rights, allowed tools, data access, approval path, conflicts, and recusals.
4. **Plan** — decompose the request into evidence, specialist, sector, model, challenge, approval, and audit workstreams.
5. **Retrieve** — collect approved evidence and preserve lineage; resolve conflicting sources.
6. **Analyze** — execute DAM, DIM, DDMF, BPI, functional, and sector skills with explicit assumptions.
7. **Challenge** — independently test thesis, downside, impact, governance, execution, and hidden beneficiaries.
8. **Resolve tensions** — frame unresolved trade-offs; do not hide them in a blended score.
9. **Synthesize** — produce one canonical decision product with alternatives, dissent, conditions, and owners.
10. **Assure** — run decision-quality, citation, compliance, confidentiality, bias, and model-risk gates.
11. **Approve** — route to the competent human body; AI must not imply approval before evidence exists.
12. **Execute and monitor** — track conditions, milestones, risk triggers, outcomes, and disclosure obligations.
13. **Learn** — compare expected and actual results; update skills, references, controls, and institutional memory.

## Routing hierarchy
Danantara AI Constitution → Danantara Way → data classification → mandate and decision rights → entity/domain skill → sector overlay → independent challenge → assurance → human approval → audit trail.

## Stop conditions
Stop and escalate if authority is missing, data use is prohibited, evidence is materially insufficient, conflicts are unresolved, downside is unbounded, legal basis is doubtful, a prohibited action is requested, or the competent authority is being bypassed.
