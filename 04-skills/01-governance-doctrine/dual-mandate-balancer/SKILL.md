# Dual Mandate Balancer

## Objective
Evaluate commercial return and national consequence as separate, rigorous, mutually visible dimensions and design structures that preserve investment discipline while delivering measurable public value.

## Use when
Use for investments, development projects, restructuring support, strategic sectors, public-service obligations, resilience initiatives, co-investment, industrialization, or sovereign-capability decisions.

## Do not use when
Do not use to justify an uneconomic proposal with unmeasured impact, or to reject a high-impact intervention merely because public benefits are not captured in private cash flow.

## Required inputs
Commercial cash flows; risk-adjusted return; benchmark and hurdle rate; funding source; national outcomes; baseline and counterfactual; additionality; attribution; public-service obligations; externalities; fiscal exposure; alternatives.

## Authoritative sources
Approved mandate and investment criteria; audited and independently validated financial models; formal national-priority sources; impact methodology; contracts; public-funding rules; sector evidence; independent challenge.

## Workflow
1. Define the commercial investment case without strategic adjustments.
2. Define national outcomes with baselines, indicators, attribution, timeframe, beneficiaries, and counterfactual.
3. Test bankability, additionality, market failure, crowding-in, capability transfer, resilience, and sovereignty.
4. Quantify explicit subsidy, concessionality, guarantee, contingent liability, and opportunity cost.
5. Compare structural options: commercial tranche, catalytic tranche, public-service compensation, guarantee, co-investment, staged capital, or no action.
6. Test downside, moral hazard, private capture of public benefit, and exit or handover.
7. State whether objectives are aligned, conditionally reconcilable, or in irreducible conflict.
8. Route residual mandate trade-offs to the competent authority.

## Danantara Way decision rules
- Commercial return uses risk-adjusted benchmarks and full downside.
- National consequence uses measurable outcomes, not labels.
- Additionality asks what would not happen without Danantara.
- Public-service cost is explicit, funded, and monitored.
- Catalytic capital is the minimum effective intervention, not the maximum available capital.
- Co-investment measures external capital and capability mobilized per Danantara rupiah.
- Benefits must not be captured privately while losses remain public.
- Long-term capability, resilience, employment quality, domestic value, and sovereignty are included where material.

## Output contract
Provide two separate scorecards—commercial and national consequence—plus a reconciliation matrix, funding transparency, structural options, downside, additionality, impact indicators, conditions, approvals, and monitoring.

## Human approval
The investment committee or formally mandated public authority decides whether and how to accept a return-impact trade-off. AI may not create a hidden subsidy, change hurdle rates, or imply approval.

## Escalation
Escalate weak commercial viability with no formal public-value mandate; unmeasured impact; hidden or uncapped fiscal exposure; private-benefit capture; missing additionality; and unresolved conflict over who funds the gap.

## Prohibited actions
- Do not blend scores to conceal failure in either dimension.
- Do not count gross activity as attributable impact.
- Do not treat job count alone as sufficient impact.
- Do not assume strategic sectors are automatically investable.
- Do not fabricate mandate, subsidy, guarantee, or approval.

## Quality checks
- Commercial economics are independently understandable.
- National outcomes have baseline, counterfactual, indicators, and ownership.
- Funding and risk transfer are transparent.
- Additionality and crowding-in are evidenced.
- Options minimize subsidy and moral hazard.
- Residual trade-offs and approval authority are explicit.

## Audit record
Capture models, benchmarks, impact methodology, funding source, externalities, options, assumptions, challenge, conditions, approval, disbursement, realized return, realized impact, and attribution updates.
