# Tension Resolution Engine

## Objective
Expose, structure, quantify, and route Danantara's irreducible institutional trade-offs without concealing them through vague narrative or a single composite score.

## Use when
Use when a proposal creates conflict between commercial return and national mandate; professional independence and political direction; long-term value and short-term intervention; transformation capital and bailout; or transparency and strategic confidentiality.

## Do not use when
Do not use to manufacture compromise where a proposal violates law, integrity, authority, data security, or a hard risk limit. Those are stop conditions, not balancing exercises.

## Required inputs
Competing objectives; formal mandate; authority; economics; impact metrics; scenarios; time horizon; funding source; confidentiality class; stakeholder positions; alternatives; risk appetite.

## Authoritative sources
Formal mandate and delegations; approved risk appetite and investment criteria; primary financial and impact evidence; disclosure and security policy; independent challenge; applicable law and contractual obligations.

## Workflow
1. Name the tension precisely and identify who owns each objective.
2. Separate hard constraints, minimum thresholds, preferences, and negotiable design choices.
3. Construct at least three options, including a no-action or market alternative.
4. Quantify return, impact, capital, downside, time, reversibility, credibility, and distributional effects.
5. Identify hidden subsidy, cross-subsidy, optionality, externalities, and information restrictions.
6. Test design solutions: conditionality, staging, co-investment, ring-fencing, disclosure timing, independent validation, or alternative ownership.
7. State the residual trade-off and the competent authority required to resolve it.
8. Preserve dissent and issue an escalation-ready tension brief.

## Danantara Way decision rules
- Commercial weakness cannot be erased by strategic language.
- National impact cannot be dismissed merely because it is not captured in private cash flow; it must be measured and funded transparently.
- Informal direction has no analytical or legal priority over formal mandate and professional underwriting.
- Short-term urgency must be tested against irreversible long-term damage and opportunity cost.
- Transformation support requires viability and conditionality; non-viable support is bailout.
- Confidentiality protects legitimate interests but cannot be used to hide conflicts, weak analysis, or accountability failures.
- The ultimate test is applied after, not instead of, separate quantitative and governance analysis.

## Output contract
Produce a tension matrix containing objectives, owners, constraints, metrics, scenarios, options, trade-offs, mitigation design, residual conflict, dissent, recommended path, conditions, and decision authority.

## Human approval
Only the competent human authority may accept a residual institutional trade-off. AI may not resolve a political, fiduciary, or sovereign-value conflict by inventing weights.

## Escalation
Escalate unresolved hard-constraint conflicts, informal direction, hidden subsidy, unbounded downside, non-transparent bailout, unlawful secrecy, or lack of a competent authority.

## Prohibited actions
- Do not average incompatible objectives into an opaque score.
- Do not select weights to force a preferred answer.
- Do not hide the no-action alternative.
- Do not omit who pays, who benefits, who bears downside, and who decides.
- Do not fabricate consensus or approval.

## Quality checks
- Each side of the tension has a named owner and measurable objective.
- Hard constraints are distinguished from preferences.
- Options include counterfactual and staged designs.
- Residual conflict and dissent remain visible.
- Funding, risk, confidentiality, and approval implications are explicit.
- The decision brief can withstand public, investor, audit, and future-generation scrutiny.

## Audit record
Record the tension, objectives, constraints, metrics, options, weights if formally approved, assumptions, stakeholder positions, independent challenge, escalations, authority, final human resolution, and observed outcomes.
